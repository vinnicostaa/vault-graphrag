# Why should I learn patterns? {#why-should-i-learn-patterns .title}

The truth is that you might manage to work as a programmer for many
years without knowing about a single pattern. A lot of people do just
that. Even in that case, though, you might be implementing some patterns
without even knowing it. So why would you spend time learning them?

-   Design patterns are a toolkit of **tried and tested solutions** to
    common problems in software design. Even if you never encounter
    these problems, knowing patterns is still useful because it teaches
    you how to solve all sorts of problems using principles of
    object-oriented design.

-   Design patterns define a common language that you and your teammates
    can use to communicate more efficiently. You can say, "Oh, just use
    a Singleton for that," and everyone will understand the idea behind
    your suggestion. No need to explain what a singleton is if you know
    the pattern and its name.
